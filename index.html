Introduction to Material #2217u6

Introduction

Material has been discovered. The Novel Advanced Processing (NAP) has defined processing pathways by which components can be manufactured. A limited set of experiments have been performed on the Material, showing anomalous electronic [1-7] and structural [8,9] properties. For example, a high elastic modulus of > 2700 GPa and the electron / hole mobilities faster than that of single crystal silicon.

The continuing experimental process will allow us to fully understand its physical and experimental properties. Below are our initial findings.

Electro-optical Properties

The electronic properties of the Material is highly anisotropic, although measurements have only been made from the two planes that cleave readily (111) and (110). The optical properties, normally expected to correlate with the electronic properties, are shown to be highly non-linear. Figure 1 shows a spherocylinder of Material, processed by a powder sintering route, interaction with photons in the visual spectrum.
