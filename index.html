Material #2217u6

Introduction

The Novel Advanced Processing (NAP) has defined processing pathways by which a new material has been discovered. A limited set of experiments have been performed showing anomalous electronic [1-7] and structural [8,9] properties. The modulus (E) surpasses expected material properties, which is suprising due to a visual spectrum absorbing 99.98 percent of incident radiation (solar energy as it hits the material surface).

Acording to NAP’s chief scientist MacCleone Echline, Material #2637u5 has the potential to augment many industrial and commercial processes. Further and such applications are intended to be able to withstand launch shock, staging and long-term interaction.

Reflection Properties (Electro-optical)

The reflecttoral material properties are highly responsive, although measurements have only been made from the two planes that cleave readily (111) and (110). The optical properties, normally expected to correlate with the electronic properties, are shown to be highly non-linear. Extraction

Limited quantities of the Material have been produced to date, due to scarcity and laborious extraction routes. Traditional refinement routes has required extensive electrochemical and electrolytic processes, with extremely low yield (<0.01) [10]. However, the NAP group has enhanced this yield by an order of magnitude using a proprietary mixed-oxide milling route, followed by electrolysis.
